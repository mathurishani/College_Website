College Day

The college celebrated its College Day on 20th march in an online mode. Due to the constraints imposed by the COVID-19 pandemic, the programme was pre-recorded by Student Union and streamed on the college’s official YouTube channel to facilitate participation of students, faculty members and all stake holders.

The program has received more than 3000 views and 385 likes so far.

The online function was graced by the Chief Guest Mr. Rajat Gupta, an alumnus of our college.

The programme commenced with a keynote address by the Advisor, Student Union of the college. She is also a faculty member in the Department of Maths of the College. A virtual tour of the college was presented with the backdrop of college invocation by the Music Society of College. The tour panned the camera through glimpses of the college infrastructure and of the many endeavors that the college partakes in throughout the year. It also showcased the Museums and Archives Learning Centre introducing the rich legacy of the college.

This was followed by the welcome address by Chairman, Governing Body of College. He appreciated the college community for being perseverant even through unprecedented times and for working dedicatedly in the fields of both academics and extracurricular activities. He highlighted the additions to the college infrastructure and commended the Madam Principal for her extensive role in the process. The programme advanced with the launch of annual college students’ magazine. The College Heritage Calendar 2021 was unveiled next, which brought to the fore faces of all the principals so far of the College.

The Principal then addressed the gathering and welcomed the honorable Chief Guest. She emphasized on the achievement of the College. She lauded the efforts of the college community through the novel experiences of the past year. She also announced that the distribution of the Annual College Prizes would be done in the college to felicitate the outstanding performance of students at the earliest possible instance.

The programme furthered with the address by Honorable Chief Guest who congratulated the institution for its rich and glorious history, heritage and culture. He motivated the audience by advising the students to never settle for mediocrity and to strive for excellence in everything. The event was concluded by the Vice President of the Students’ Union, who extended a vote of thanks to the esteemed guests and faculty for their support and presence on this special occasion as the college. The national anthem was then played to bring the event to a close. The programme was successful and was met with active participation by the college community; it promulgated a message in resilience, unity and perseverance.
